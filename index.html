Research

His research focuses on understanding how humans interact with and percieve social robots and what consequences do these interactions and perceptions have on the interacting human. His work examines the cognitive and neural mechanisms underlying human-robot interaction as it relates to visual attention, decision making, perceptual judgments, and reward processing. He takes an integrative and multi-level approach that makes use of several techniques, including the electroencephalogram, neuroimaging, brain stimulation, and behavioral paradigms.
